Tribut Page

payment made periodically by one state or ruler to another, especially as a sign of dependence.

About Tributes

  • In case of alliances, lesser parties may pay tribute to more powerful parties as a sign of allegiance and often in order to finance projects that would benefit both parties
  • The ancient Persian Achaemenid Empire is an example of an ancient tribute empire; one that made relatively few demands on its non-Persian subjects other than the regular payment of tribute, which might be gold, luxury goods, animals, soldiers or slaves.
  • The medieval Mongol rulers of Russia also expected only tribute from the Russian states, which continued to govern themselves
  • The reliefs at Persepolis show processions of figures bearing varied types of tribute
